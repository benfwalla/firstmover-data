I spent way too much time on StreetEasy this week hunting for apartments at exactly $2,500/month. One in each borough. Here's what I found.

## Manhattan: A Miracle

Finding anything at $2,500 in Manhattan feels like winning the lottery. After scrolling through pages of "price reduced from $4,500," I found exactly one option:

A 300 square foot studio on St. Marks Place. No kitchen to speak of, shower in the corner, and you can touch both walls with your arms spread. But hey, it's Manhattan.

## Brooklyn: The Sweet Spot

Brooklyn at $2,500 is where things get interesting. Options in Crown Heights, Bed-Stuy, and even some parts of Bushwick.

## Queens: The Value Champion

Queens is where your money actually stretches. $2,500 gets you legitimate space and amenities.

## The Bronx: Hidden Potential

The Bronx at $2,500 feels like cheating. You get space, amenities, and neighborhoods that are rapidly changing.

## Staten Island: A Different Planet

Staten Island at $2,500 is basically suburban living with a NYC zip code.

Three bedrooms, two bathrooms, and a view of the harbor. The catch? Everything requires a car, and getting to Manhattan involves a ferry ride.

## The Verdict

Your $2,500 budget determines your entire NYC experience. Manhattan gets you location but no space. Brooklyn and Queens offer the best balance. The Bronx gives you the most bang for your buck. Staten Island... exists.

Choose your poison wisely.
