## Analysis

January's surge to 17,412 new listings represents the largest monthly influx since summer 2023. Yet median rent barely budged from December's levels.

Williamsburg topped the volume charts with 683 new apartments, followed by Bushwick at 655. This concentration in north Brooklyn reflects new construction coming online, not organic market expansion.

The bedroom breakdown tells the real story. One-bedrooms dominated with 6,991 listings at $3,427 median. Two-bedrooms numbered 5,359 at $3,995. Studios remained scarce — just 2,873 units.

Neighborhood price movements varied dramatically. Saint George, Hudson Square, and Red Hook saw significant drops, while others increased.

## What's Next

February will test whether January's inventory surge represents a lasting shift or seasonal adjustment. Watch for continued new construction deliveries in Williamsburg and Long Island City.
